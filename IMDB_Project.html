Exploring Viewer Preferences and Industry Dynamics in the World of Streaming

Introduction

In today's world of streaming services, where an abundance of shows and movies captivate audiences with their diverse content, understanding what makes a title popular and engaging for viewers is crucial. This analysis delves into a dataset that includes content from well-known streaming platforms like Netflix, Disney, Paramount, and HBO. We use the IMDB ratings in this dataset as a measure of how audiences react to this content, with a focus on exploring how these ratings relate to when these movies and shows were released and the genres they belong to. Our project aims to offer valuable insights through carefully crafted research questions, visualizations, and rigorous statistical analyses, all aimed at uncovering viewer preferences and the dynamics of the entertainment industry.

 

Assessing IMDB Ratings and Audience Preferences

While the IMDB rating serves as a crucial indicator for gauging the show's quality and our audience's preferences, it is essential to assess the reliability of these ratings. The first approach is to evaluate the level of viewer participation in contributing to the overall rating, the average number of IMDB votes for each movie is 10,476, the number of participations within the dataset demonstrates a fair reliability to reflect the broader audience's preferences. One-hot encoding method was employed to generate additional logical columns for each movie genre, along with a column to identify whether a movie or TV show has a unique genre.   

 

Age group is an understanding preference of adult viewers versus general audiences is crucial for content creators and marketers to tailor their strategies to target specific viewer groups, as adult-targeted content often engages with different themes, tones, and complexities compared to content aimed at a broader, younger audience. Although the dataset lacks specific values for age groups, it provides age ratings for each movie, which have been condensed into a binary category of adult/non -adult, this distinction aims to analyze audience preferences based on content maturity.

 

Missing Data Imputation

Following the model creation and training on the complete data subset, regression model is applied to the incomplete data subset to predict and impute missing binary values in the age classification column. The caret package (Classification and Regression Training) function is used in the process to streamline the model training process of address missing binary data within a dataset systematically and with controlled precision, providing a valuable tool for data imputation in classification tasks.

The scatter plot's trend line reveals a subtle positive correlation between release year and IMDb score, suggesting that, on average, more recent movies tend to receive slightly higher IMDb ratings. However, it's important to note that this relationship is relatively weak, indicating that other factors likely play a substantial role in determining a movie's IMDb score. A notable shift in movie density occurs around 2005, signifying a significant change in the industry. The increased production output has both positive and negative consequences for viewers and the industry. On the positive side, the mass production of movies and TV shows has led to a greater variety of content, catering to diverse viewer preferences. This surge in production has enabled viewers to access a wide range of genres and styles, contributing to a richer and more dynamic entertainment landscape. However, this production boom also results in varying quality levels. While there are undoubtedly excellent productions, the sheer volume of content means that there is a higher likelihood of encountering movies and TV shows of lower quality. Viewers must navigate through a mix of outstanding and subpar content, making informed choices and relying on sources like IMDb ratings to guide their selections.

When attempting to fit a simple linear regression model with the IMDB score as the dependent variable and release year as the independent variable to the dataset, a significant issue arose.  The estimated intercept of negative value (-1.38) raised concerns as this interpretation lacked practical significance, given that the IMDB score range from 1 to 10, and the intercept should not be lower than zero. To address this concern, the release year variable was recentered by subtracting the smallest year (1901) from all release year values. This adjustment allowed for a more meaningful interpretation of the model. Following the recentering, the coefficient indicated that for each unit increase in the release year, there would be an expected average increase of 0.004 units in the IMDB score. This finding suggests a relatively weak relationship between these variables. Notably, when the release year is set at 1901, the predicted IMDB score stands at 6.18. The presence of unbalanced skewness in the residuals might account for the observed weak correlation. Outliers that deviate significantly from the overall trend have the potential to weaken the linear relationship. The p-value of 0.00086 suggests a significant relationship between IMDB ratings and release year, rendering the model statistically significant. However, the slightly unbalanced skewness of the residuals may still contribute to the weakened correlation due to the presence of such outliers. In summary, the results of this linear regression model demonstrate that IMDB ratings are influenced by a multitude of factors. While there is a positive correlation between IMDB ratings and release year, the impact of the release year itself appears to be relatively small. The relationship is somewhat weakened by outliers and other factors, suggesting that other variables play a more substantial role in determining IMDB ratings.

Random Intercept

The analysis conducted figure 5a centers around a linear regression model which incorporates random intercepts in genres, This model is a linear regression model that aims to predict imdb_score based on two predictor variables: release_year and genres. However, it includes a random intercept term for genres, indicating that it's accounting for random variation among different genres. The (1|genres) term helps model the random effects of genres on the intercept (the baseline IMDB score) while allowing release_year to have a fixed effect on imdb_score.

Diving into the findings of the fixed effect, it unveils a somewhat unexpected discovery. While we introduce the random intercept of genre into the model, taking into account that different genres may have different baseline IMDB scores, it appears to have a moderating effect on the relationship between the IMDB score and the release year. Surprisingly, this relationship displays a negative correlation. In other words, as movies and TV shows in different genres are massively produced, some of the genre IMDB ratings tend to dip and influence the whole trending. This insight suggests that newer isn't always better in the eyes of the IMDB audience. To support the reliability of these theory, we need to examine the model evaluation, as indicated by the relatively low AIC and BIC values, the model fitting appears promising. The majority of residuals exhibit only minor variations compared to a simpler linear regression model. This implies that our mixed-effects model is capturing the essential dynamics in the data without introducing excessive complexity. Now, let's delve into the intriguing concept of the Intra-class Correlation Coefficient (ICC). This statistic assesses the degree of similarity or variance within groups compared to the total variability. In our case, it gauges how much of the total variation in IMDB ratings can be attributed to differences between genres. The ICC value of 0.2098 suggests that about 20.98% of the overall variance in IMDB ratings can be attributed to the distinctions between genres. This might be due to unique genre-related preferences or characteristics that affect audience ratings. The random effect is not too significant comparing to the result of the fixed effect, meaning that the model still need improvement.

Random Intercept and Slope

Figure 5 indicates the summary of a linear model include both random intercepts and slopes for release year within genre, capturing variations in the baseline IMDB scores and how IMDB scores change with release year within each genre. It assesses how the effects of release year on IMDB scores can differ among different genres, making it a more complex and flexible model than the previous one. Most of the results are similar to the last model.   Looking at the fixed effect, it reveals a statistically significant relationship (lies in the confidence interval 5%) between IMDb ratings and the release year, with a negative trend as well. For the random effect, intercept for "genres" has a variance of 0.06243 and a standard deviation of approximately 0.2499, indicating substantial variability in the baseline IMDB scores among different genres. The random slope for release year within genres has a variance of 1.531e-05 and a standard deviation of approximately 0.003913, suggesting a limited variability in how IMDB scores change with centered release year within genres. Perfect correlation means that as the baseline IMDB score varies among genres, the effect of centered release year also varies in a consistent manner. In general , this analysis indicates that the model provides a better fit compared to the previous model , and a marginally significant level indicating the relationship of IMDB score and release year, a weak effect of deviation in the overall relationship.

Likelihood testing

To explore the potential influence of viewer age on IMDb ratings in conjunction with release year, we initially observed that our dataset did not directly contain age group factors. To address this, we aggregated the various age limitations of movies into a binary category, distinguishing between "for adult" and "not for adult" classifications. This approach allowed us to create a meaningful variable indicating the intended audience for each movie or TV show. Now, in this analysis, we seek to examine how this binary "for adult" classification, in combination with release year, impacts IMDb ratings across different genres. We will be using linear mixed-effects models to investigate these relationships. Specifically, we will compare two models: one with a random intercept for genres (imdblmer3) and another with both a random intercept and a random slope for genres concerning the centered release year (imdblmer4). By comparing these models using an analysis of variance (ANOVA), we aim to discern whether the inclusion of viewer age, represented by the "for adult" variable, significantly affects our understanding of how IMDb ratings relate to release year and genre.

Null Hypothesis (H0): There is no significant difference in the fit of the linear mixed-effects models in explaining the variability in IMDB ratings.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1): At least one of the models provides a significantly better fit than the others in explaining the relationship between IMDB ratings, centered release year, genres, and the "for adult" variable.

Result of the likelihood result test, the model including both random intercepts and slopes for release year within genre, which assess how different genres not only have different average IMDB score values. With further random effects, yields a Chisq value of 5.413915, leading to a p-value of 0.019, which lies in the 5 percent confidence interval. This result conclude that that include the “adult or not” classifications might increase the model complexity,  but lower the interpretability and practical significance.

Limitations

It is important to acknowledge that this dataset may not offer a comprehensive representation of the broader entertainment industry. Possible selection biases in the content included within the dataset could introduce a degree of skewness to the results. Although the dataset lacks detailed information about the specific average age of viewers who contribute to IMDB ratings, it employs age restriction levels as a proxy to estimate the potential impact of viewer age on show ratings. It is worth noting that the outcomes of the linear regression model including the (adult/non-adult) category suggest that it might not be a reliable indicator for assessing the relationship between IMDB ratings and release year.

Conclusion

In conclusion, this analysis shed light on the complex interplay of genres, IMDb ratings, release years in the context of streaming content. It highlighted the industry's evolution, the impact of mass production, and the intricate relationships that affect IMDb ratings. From the exploration of the data set we have observed that certain genres, such as "Documentation," "Drama," and "Sci-Fi," dominate the content landscape, collectively constituting a substantial portion of the dataset. Furthermore, our analysis unveiled an evolving trend in genre distribution over time. While genres like "Documentation" and "Drama" maintained their prominence, there was a notable surge in production after 2010. This indicates a thriving entertainment industry post-2009 and a diversification of genres to cater to evolving audience preferences. Presence of outliers in genres like "Action" and "History" demonstrated that the average ratings of these genres deviated significantly from the overall mean. To gain a deeper insight, multiple hierarchical models were compared, and the result revealed a negative correlation between IMDb ratings and release years when the genre factor was included. Ultimately, our findings emphasize that when the release year of the movie/TV show is later, the IMDB score tends to be lower while including the effect of different genre. This finding confirms the mass production creates an increasing variation in genres, which caused both positive and negative consequences. On one hand, it diversified content, offering a broader range of genres and styles for audience to choose. On the other hand, it also results in large variation in the poor production quality levels, causing the overall IMDB rating to drop.
